The intend of this page is to establish a list of equipment known to work with RoadMap. The list is still short, mostly because the page has just been established.

This page includes links to manufacturer's product pages. This site has no commercial agreement and made no advertisement deal with the vendors. These links are provided for technical information purpose only.

Please do not hesitate to share your experience by sending an e-mail to the RoadMap mailing list.

It is expected that RoadMap will work with most NMEA-compatible GPS receivers. A frequent source of compatibility problem is the support for USB devices in the Linux kernel, as some GPS USB receivers do not seem to use a standard USB communication device model.


Host Computers:

  • IBM PC and compatible running Linux or FreeBSD.
  • HP (formely Compaq) iPAQ running Familiar/Linux. Please check the Handhelds.org site for more information on supported models.
  • Sharp Zaurus.

GPS Receivers Tested With RoadMap:

  • Garmin 38 (now obsolete), and probably most Garmin GPS devices.
  • Rayming TripNav TN204 (for PDAs).

GPS Receivers said to work with gpsd:

  • GPS devices known to work with Gpsdrive (GpsDrive uses gpsd as well)
